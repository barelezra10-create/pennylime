01 · The Story

Built for the way
platforms pay.

Sixty million Americans now run small businesses inside platforms. They drive for Uber and Lyft. They deliver for DoorDash, Instacart, and Amazon Flex. They sell on Amazon, Shopify, and Etsy. They take gigs on Fiverr, Upwork, TaskRabbit, and Rover. The platform is the storefront, the app is the back office, and the bank deposit is the receipt.

The financial system never adapted. Banks still ask for a W-2. Underwriting models still optimize for predictable salary income. Repayment terms still assume you get paid every other Friday by the same employer for thirty years.

So we built PennyLime. We read 90 days of bank deposits the way the old system used to read a pay stub. We size funding based on what you actually earn. We collect repayment as a percentage of your future earnings, not a fixed monthly bill, because the work itself isn't fixed.

If you drive, deliver, sell, or ship, we fund you. In 48 hours.

02 · Mission & Values

What we believe.

Mission

Fund the people who make the on-demand economy run, on terms that respect what they actually earn.

Vision

A world where a Tuesday-night Uber driver, an Amazon FBA seller, and a Fiverr designer can all access capital as easily and fairly as a Fortune 500 company.

Promise

Your platform earnings are your credit. We disclose every cost in plain English before you accept. We say no when no is the right answer.

Values

Plain English

Factor rate. Daily remittance. Total cost. Spelled out before you tap accept. No fine print, no asterisks, no surprise fees.

Earnings > Credit

90 days of bank deposits is a better predictor than a FICO score for a 1099 worker or platform seller. We underwrite reality, not paperwork.

Fast or it doesn't count

Money you need next week is a different product than money you need tomorrow. We optimize for now, because that's when it matters.

Decline kindly

If we can't fund you safely, we'll say so within minutes and explain why. Hope is not a product.

Tech, not theater

Plaid for income. Increase for ACH. Real APIs running real underwriting. Not a 1990s underwriter in a startup t-shirt.

Repayment is a relationship

Slow week? Your remittance is a percentage of receivables, not a fixed bill. The product moves with you. Reward repeat customers.

03 · Positioning

Where we sit.

PennyLime is a tech-driven funding platform for the on-demand workforce. Our product is a merchant cash advance, but our positioning is a software company. We earn customer trust the way Stripe earned developer trust: with transparency, predictable APIs, and a brand that signals competence and warmth at the same time.

Positioning Statement

For drivers, sellers, and operators who earn through platforms and can't get a fair shake from traditional lenders, PennyLime is the tech-driven funding partner that buys a piece of your future earnings at a transparent rate, deposits cash within 48 hours, and adapts when your week does. Unlike payday lenders, generic MCAs, or banks, we underwrite from real-time bank data and price for the actual risk of the income, not the absence of a W-2.

We are
  • A tech company that ships funding
  • A receivables-purchase product (MCA)
  • API-driven and audit-ready
  • Fair, fast, transparent
  • Built specifically for platform earnings
We are not
  • A bank or licensed lender
  • A payday loan
  • A generic MCA chasing restaurants
  • A credit-score-driven product
  • "Lending for the underbanked"

06 · The Lime & The Wordmark

A lime. A name.
A period.

Two pieces. The lime mark for compact contexts (favicon, app icon, social avatar, swag). The wordmark for everywhere there's room. They never appear together as a forced lockup, because each does the job alone.

The lime mark. A half-slice cross-section of a lime, illustrated with warmth: bumpy rind, eight visible segments, juicy translucent flesh, white pith. Sits at a slight tilt with a soft ground shadow. Friendly, edible, unmistakable.

PennyLime.

The wordmark. "Penny" in ink, "Lime" plus the trailing period in brand green. Inter 800, letter-spacing -5%. The period turns the wordmark into a sentence: declarative, finished, confident. Never set in all caps.

Always reserve a margin equal to one-third of the mark's height on all sides. Nothing — type, image, edge — encroaches.

Minimum sizes

  • Crop the lime tighter than its natural canvas

09 · Iconography

Two-pixel rule.

Icons are drawn with a 2-pixel stroke, rounded caps, and rounded joins. No fills. No multi-color icons. They live in a 24-pixel grid and align to whole pixels at common UI sizes. The vibe is engineering, not illustration. The lime mark is the only allowed exception.

10 · Photography & Illustration

Real people.
Real platforms.

PennyLime never uses smiling-stock-actor handshake photography. Our visual world is two layers: documentary photography of platform work in progress, and the lime mark as the signature illustration accent. Editorial illustration is permitted for product moments only.

Documentary, candid, available light. Driver's seat at 6am, delivery bag on a sidewalk, an Amazon FBA seller's desk, the moment of a bank notification.
Photography · Documentary. Cool color cast. People working at platforms, never posing. Avoid handshakes, board rooms, white-toothed smiles.
The lime mark · Sits on warm paper or ink. Gets the spotlight. Never crowded by other illustrations.

Illustration system rules

The lime is the star

Editorial illustrations support the lime, never compete with it. The lime gets the warmth; everything else stays geometric.

Two-color limit

Brand green plus one neutral for editorial illustration. Tints/shades of green allowed. The lime mark is the only multicolor exception.

Show the artifact

Phones, app screens, bank statements, receipts, dashboards. Illustrate the artifact of the transaction, not the handshake.

11 · Motion Principles

Confident,
not hyperactive.

Movement signals technology and momentum, but never desperation. PennyLime motion is decisive: short durations, ease-out curves, no lingering bounces or attention-seeking loops.

Duration

Standard transitions 220ms. Page changes 340ms. Status confirmations 450ms with overshoot.

Easing

ease-out for entries. ease-in for exits. Never linear, never bouncy.

Direction

Forward motion only on success. Backward motion reserved for errors and reversals. Vertical for steps in a flow; horizontal for time.

Signature interactions

  • Approval moment: The lime mark briefly grows 8% then settles, with a soft ground-shadow pulse. The status badge ticks to "Approved."
  • Number reveal: Currency values count from 0 to final on first paint over 600ms. They do not animate on subsequent loads.
  • Step progression: Vertical scroll between steps. Each step's headline fades in on entry (220ms), supporting copy 80ms after.
  • Success disbursement: A single particle of brand green travels from the bank icon to the user's account icon along a smooth path. One time, never looped.

14 · Don'ts

If you do these,
we are no longer us.

Don't

Call the product a "loan"

It is a merchant cash advance, the purchase of future receivables. The legal distinction matters and the brand reinforces it everywhere.

Don't

Use APR or interest-rate language

Cost is expressed as a factor rate. Quoting an APR triggers regulatory exposure and confuses the customer.

Don't

Show stock photos of suits shaking hands

Our customers don't wear suits. Use documentary photography of actual gig and platform work, or system-styled illustration.

Don't

Use "easy money" or pity language

We don't sell desperation. Our copy treats the customer as a smart business operator facing a normal capital need.

Don't

Crop, recolor, or restyle the lime mark

The lime is the only multicolor illustration in the system. Modifying it dilutes the only visual moat we have.

Don't

Hide the total cost

Total dollar cost is shown above the fold of every approval screen. No fine print, no asterisks. Transparency is the product.

When in doubt: simpler, clearer, less. Ask "would I trust this if I drove for Uber, sold on Amazon, or freelanced on Fiverr?" before shipping anything that says "PennyLime."
