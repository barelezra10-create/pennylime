01 · The Story

Same job.
New rules.

The bank was built for a world where you got paid every other Friday by the same employer for thirty years. That world is gone. Sixty million Americans now earn their living one ride, one delivery, one project at a time. But the financial system still asks for the W-2 it knows will never come.

PennyLime exists because someone had to design funding for the way work actually happens. Not "alternative" lending. Not a workaround. A funding system that reads bank deposits the same way an old loan officer read a pay stub, except faster, fairer, and on a phone.

We're not a bank. We're not a payday lender. We're not a generic merchant-cash-advance company chasing brick-and-mortar restaurants. We are a tech company building the receivables-purchase product the gig economy needed ten years ago.

02 · Mission & Values

What we believe.

Mission

Fund the people who fund the on-demand economy, on terms that respect what they actually earn.

Vision

A world where a Tuesday-night Uber driver can get capital to fix their car as fast as a Fortune 500 can issue a corporate bond, and at a fair price.

Promise

Your earnings are your credit. We never ask for a W-2. We disclose every cost in plain English before you accept. We say no when no is the right answer.

Values

Plain English

Factor rate. Daily remittance. Total cost. Spelled out before you tap accept. No fine print, no last-minute surprise fees.

Earnings > Credit

A 92-day deposit history is a better predictor than a FICO score for a 1099 worker. We underwrite reality, not paperwork.

Fast or it doesn't count

Money you need next week is a different product than money you need tomorrow. We optimize for now, because that's when it matters.

Decline kindly

If we can't fund you safely, we'll say so within minutes and explain why. Hope is not a product.

Tech, not theater

Plaid for income. Increase for ACH. Real APIs running real underwriting. Not a 1990s underwriter in a startup t-shirt.

Repayment is a relationship

We model hardship into the product. Slow week? Your remittance is a percentage, not a fixed bill. Reward repeat customers.

03 · Positioning

Where we sit.

PennyLime is a tech-first funding platform for the on-demand workforce. Our product is a merchant cash advance, but our positioning is a software company. We earn customer trust the way Stripe earned developer trust, with developer-grade transparency, predictable APIs, and a brand that signals competence.

Positioning Statement

For self-employed gig workers and 1099 earners who can't get a fair shake from traditional lenders, PennyLime is the tech-driven funding partner that buys a piece of your future earnings at a transparent rate, deposits cash within 48 hours, and adapts when your week does. Unlike payday lenders, generic MCAs, or banks, we underwrite from real-time bank data and price for the actual risk of the income, not the absence of a W-2.

We are
  • A tech company that ships funding
  • A receivables-purchase product (MCA)
  • API-driven and audit-ready
  • Fair, fast, transparent
  • Built specifically for 1099 income
We are not
  • A bank or licensed lender
  • A payday loan
  • A generic MCA chasing restaurants
  • A credit-score-driven product
  • "Lending for the underbanked"

04 · The Customer

Who we build for.

Our customer is not a type of person. It's a type of work. The Uber driver in Tampa, the freelance designer in Brooklyn, the rover sitter in Phoenix, the Amazon Flex driver in Sacramento — they share an income pattern, not a demographic.

Primary

The Working Operator

25-55 years old. Drives 30-60 hours a week across 1-3 platforms. Income $2K-$8K monthly. Owns a phone, a vehicle, sometimes a small fleet. Treats their gig as a serious business; tracks miles in an app, files a Schedule C. Needs $500-$5,000 advances for vehicle repair, equipment, or to bridge a slow week.

ChannelReddit communities, platform driver forums, TikTok
PainGets declined by banks; suspicious of payday lenders
TriggerTruck breaks down, slow holiday week, child medical bill
Secondary

The Sole Proprietor

28-50. Freelance creative, consultant, or platform service provider (Fiverr, Upwork, TaskRabbit, Thumbtack). Variable monthly income, often LLC-organized. Wants $2K-$10K to bridge AR gaps, buy equipment, or fund a marketing push.

ChannelTwitter, LinkedIn, freelancer Discords
PainIncome is "lumpy"; banks want 2 years of tax returns
TriggerBig invoice 60 days out, software/tool needed today
What they want from us
1.

A real number, fast. Don't waste their time.

2.

Respect for their work. Stop asking for a W-2.

3.

Fair pricing in plain English. Show the total cost.

4.

Flexibility when life happens. Variable remittance is the gift.

06 · The Logo System

A lime, sliced.

The PennyLime mark is a stylized cross-section of a lime: a green hemisphere divided by six radial spokes, with a center dot. It reads as a fruit, a coin, a compass, and a sun. Read whichever feels right — they all map to "energy, freshness, direction."

Primary · Full color on white
Knockout · Single color on paper
Inverse · Bright green on ink

Wordmark

PennyLime

Type set in display sans, weight 800. The "Lime" in green; the "Penny" in ink. No space between the two halves. Never set in all-caps.

Lockup

PennyLime

Mark and wordmark together. Mark height equals 1.2× cap height. Spacing between mark and wordmark equals half the cap height (the "x").

06 · The Logo System

Clear space & sizing.

Clear space

Always reserve a margin equal to the height of the inner circle (the "x") on all sides of the mark. Nothing — type, image, edge — encroaches.

Minimum sizes

Mark must remain legible. Below these thresholds, use the simplified single-color version.

Print14pt / 5mm height min
Digital24px height min
FaviconSingle-color version only
EmbroiderySingle-color, simplified spokes

Don't

  • Stretch, skew, rotate, or recolor
  • Add drop shadows or outlines
  • Place on busy photographs without an underlay
  • Change the spoke count or center dot
  • Substitute the wordmark font

07 · Color

Ratios & rules.

Default ratio

In any composition, aim for roughly 70% neutral (paper or ink), 25% green family, and 5% accent or semantic color. Green is a finishing move, not the whole canvas.

Neutral · 70%
Green · 25%

Accessibility

All primary combinations meet or exceed WCAG AA. The brand never relies on color alone for status — pair with iconography or text.

Green / White5.1:1 · AA · use for buttons
Green / Mist4.6:1 · AA · use for badges
Ink / Paper17.4:1 · AAA · default body
Bright / Ink8.5:1 · AAA · ink-bg headlines

Don'ts

Don't blend brand green with non-system colors.

Don't reduce opacity to fake a tint. Use Mist or Pale instead.

Don't use a yellow-shifted "lime" green from outside the system.

09 · Iconography

Two-pixel rule.

Icons are drawn with a 2-pixel stroke, rounded caps, and rounded joins. No fills. No multi-color icons. They live in a 24-pixel grid and align to whole pixels at common UI sizes. The vibe is engineering, not illustration.

10 · Photography & Illustration

Real people.
Real machines.

PennyLime never uses smiling-stock-actor handshake photography. Our visual world is two layers: documentary photography of people working (delivery bags, dashboards, laptops, hands) and clean editorial illustration that shows the product itself in action.

Documentary, candid, available light. Driver's seat at 6am, delivery bag on a sidewalk, the moment of a bank notification.
Photography · Documentary. Cool color cast. People working, never posing. Avoid handshakes, board rooms, white-toothed smiles.
Illustration · Editorial geometry. Receipts, app screens, simple objects on warm paper. Always says "money moving" or "decision made."

Illustration system rules

Geometry first

Compose from rectangles, circles, and 30°/60° angles. Hand-drawn squiggles are permitted as accent only.

Two-color limit

Brand green plus one neutral. Never a rainbow palette. Tints/shades of green are allowed.

Show the product

Phones, app screens, bank statements, receipts, dashboards. We illustrate the artifact of the transaction, not the handshake.

11 · Motion Principles

Confident,
not hyperactive.

Movement signals technology and momentum, but never desperation. PennyLime motion is decisive: short durations, ease-out curves, no lingering bounces or attention-seeking loops.

Duration

Standard transitions 220ms. Page changes 340ms. Status confirmations 450ms with overshoot.

Easing

ease-out for entries. ease-in for exits. Never linear, never bouncy.

Direction

Forward motion only on success. Backward motion reserved for errors and reversals. Vertical for steps in a flow; horizontal for time.

Signature interactions

  • Approval moment: The status ring fills counter-clockwise, then locks with a single soft pulse and a check icon.
  • Number reveal: Currency values count from 0 to final on first paint over 600ms. They do not animate on subsequent loads.
  • Step progression: Vertical scroll between steps. Each step's headline fades in on entry (220ms), supporting copy 80ms after.
  • Success disbursement: A single particle of brand green travels from the bank icon to the user's account icon along a smooth path. One time, never looped.

14 · Don'ts

If you do these,
we are no longer us.

Don't

Call the product a "loan"

It is a merchant cash advance, the purchase of future receivables. The legal distinction matters and the brand reinforces it everywhere.

Don't

Use APR or interest-rate language

Cost is expressed as a factor rate. Quoting an APR triggers regulatory exposure and confuses the customer.

Don't

Show stock photos of suits shaking hands

Our customers don't wear suits. Use documentary photography of actual gig work, or system-styled illustration.

Don't

Use "easy money" or pity language

We don't sell desperation. Our copy treats the customer as a smart business owner facing a normal capital need.

Don't

Replace the brand color with a market-trending palette

Lime green is unique in the MCA space. Drifting toward fintech blue or violet erodes the only visual moat we have.

Don't

Hide the total cost

Total dollar cost is shown above the fold of every approval screen. No fine print, no asterisks. Transparency is the product.

When in doubt: simpler, clearer, less. Ask "would I trust this if I drove for Uber?" before shipping anything that says "PennyLime."
