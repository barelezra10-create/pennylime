### 1. SALE OF FUTURE RECEIVABLES (NOT A CREDIT TRANSACTION)

Merchant agrees to **sell**, and PennyLime agrees to **buy**, a defined dollar amount of Merchant's future revenue stream (the **"Purchased Receivables"**) in exchange for a one-time payment from PennyLime to Merchant (the **"Purchase Price"**).

The Merchant will deliver the Purchased Receivables to PennyLime by remitting a **Specified Percentage** of each future Payment Transaction the Merchant collects, until PennyLime has received the full Purchased Receivables amount.

**Defined terms:**

- **"Payment Transaction"** means any payment received by Merchant in the regular course of business — including cash, check, ACH, wire, card payment, RCC (remotely created check), platform payout (e.g. Uber, DoorDash, Amazon Flex), or any other monetary inflow.
- **"Specified Percentage"** is the percentage of each Payment Transaction the Merchant remits to PennyLime, fixed at **[Specified Percentage]%** for this transaction.
- **"Purchase Price"** is the amount PennyLime pays Merchant, fixed at **$[Disbursed Amount]**.
- **"Purchased Receivables"** is the total amount Merchant will deliver to PennyLime over time, fixed at **$[Total Receivables]**.

**This is not a credit product.** Merchant is selling a portion of future revenue at a discount. PennyLime is not lending money. There is no interest, no annual percentage rate, no fixed repayment schedule, and no fixed term over which the Purchased Receivables must be delivered. Merchant agrees to record this transaction as a sale of receivables (not a borrowing) in its books, tax filings, and any audited statements.

Merchant waives any future right to argue, in litigation or arbitration, that this transaction was anything other than a sale of future receivables, including any related rights of taxpayer privilege, confidentiality, or privacy that would prevent disclosure of records relevant to that defense.

### 3. ACH AUTHORIZATION

By signing this Agreement, Merchant **expressly authorizes** PennyLime (and any servicer, agent, or assign of PennyLime) to initiate electronic debits via the Automated Clearing House (ACH) network, debit-card pulls, or remotely created checks (RCC) against the **Merchant Bank Account** (defined in the Electronic Payment Authorization attached to this Agreement) on the schedule and in the amounts described in Section 2.

Merchant authorizes PennyLime to:

1. Debit the Specified Percentage of expected Payment Transactions each remittance period until the full Purchased Receivables amount is delivered.
2. Resubmit any returned debit, in compliance with NACHA Operating Rules.
3. Correct any processing error by initiating a reversing or correcting entry.
4. Apply the same authorization to any successor or replacement bank account, subject to PennyLime's prior written approval.

If a debit is rejected for non-sufficient funds or any other reason (other than a stop-payment order Merchant places without PennyLime's consent), the failure may be treated as an Event of Default per Section 7.

The Merchant Bank Account, account holder identity, and routing/account numbers are captured in the Electronic Payment Authorization. Merchant represents that the bank account is owned by Merchant and that the individual signing this Agreement has authority to bind Merchant and to authorize debits.

### 4. RECONCILIATION

The scheduled remittance amount in Section 2 is PennyLime's estimate of the Specified Percentage of Merchant's typical Payment Transactions. Because actual Payment Transactions fluctuate, Merchant may request a **Reconciliation** to true-up the debited amount to the actual Specified Percentage of receivables collected during the calendar month in question.

A Reconciliation request must be:

1. In writing;
2. Sent to PennyLime within thirty (30) days after the close of the calendar month in question;
3. Accompanied by a complete copy of all Merchant Bank Account statements for that month;
4. Sent to PennyLime at `info@pennylime.com` or to PennyLime's mailing address on file.

If the request is timely and complete, PennyLime will credit or debit the difference within ten (10) business days. **Notwithstanding the foregoing,** if an Event of Default has occurred and not been cured, the Specified Percentage automatically rises to **one hundred percent (100%)** of all incoming Payment Transactions until the full Purchased Receivables amount has been delivered.

### 5. MERCHANT REPRESENTATIONS AND COVENANTS

Merchant represents, warrants, and covenants the following as of the date of this Agreement and throughout its term:

**5.1 Authority.** Merchant is a valid legal entity in good standing in the jurisdiction of its organization, and the individual signer has authority to bind Merchant.

**5.2 Good faith operations.** Merchant will operate its business in good faith and use commercially reasonable efforts to generate the future Payment Transactions from which the Purchased Receivables will be delivered.

**5.3 Bank account stability.** Merchant will continue to deposit all Payment Transactions into the Merchant Bank Account (or such replacement account PennyLime approves in writing in advance). Merchant will not change banks, processors, or payout destinations without PennyLime's prior written consent.

**5.4 Financial information.** Merchant has furnished bank statements, payout reports, and any other documents truthfully and accurately. Merchant will furnish additional bank statements or financial records within five (5) business days of any PennyLime request.

**5.5 No stacking.** During the term of this Agreement, Merchant will not enter into any other receivables purchase, factoring, advance-against-receipts, or similar arrangement with any third party that relates to or encumbers the Future Receivables, without PennyLime's prior written consent. Any pre-existing such arrangements were fully disclosed to PennyLime in writing before this Agreement was executed.

**5.6 No bankruptcy in contemplation.** As of the date of this Agreement, Merchant is solvent, is not insolvent, has not filed and has not had filed against it any bankruptcy petition, and has not consulted with a bankruptcy attorney in the prior six months. Merchant does not presently intend to cease operations within the next six months.

**5.7 Licensing.** Merchant holds, and will maintain, all permits and licenses necessary to operate its business lawfully in every jurisdiction where it operates.

**5.8 Insurance.** Merchant will maintain such insurance as is customary for its industry and will provide proof upon request.

**5.9 Business purpose.** Merchant will use the Purchase Price for business purposes only — not for personal, household, or family purposes. Merchant understands that this representation is material to PennyLime's decision to enter into this Agreement, and that consumer-protection laws applicable to personal credit are not intended to and do not apply here.

**5.10 No conflicting obligations.** Execution of this Agreement does not violate or cause a default under any other contract Merchant is a party to.

**5.11 Bank account ownership.** The Merchant Bank Account is owned by Merchant; the signer is an authorized signatory on that account.

**5.12 Truthfulness.** All information provided to PennyLime — including the application, supporting documents, statements, written or verbal answers to questions, and any data shared electronically — is true, complete, and accurate. **Any material misrepresentation may constitute fraud, intentional misrepresentation, and unjust enrichment, entitling PennyLime to recover its losses, costs, and reasonable attorneys' fees.**

### 6. PURCHASER'S ACKNOWLEDGMENT

PennyLime acknowledges that this transaction involves risk: Merchant's business may slow, fail, or cease operating through no fault of Merchant. If Merchant's business genuinely fails or significantly slows, and Merchant has not breached this Agreement, **Merchant has no obligation to deliver Purchased Receivables beyond what its actual Payment Transactions allow.** PennyLime accepts this risk in consideration for the Purchase Price discount.

This Section 6 does not apply if Merchant breaches the Agreement, commits fraud, or triggers an Event of Default.

### 7. EVENTS OF DEFAULT AND REMEDIES

**7.1 Events of Default.** Any of the following is an "Event of Default":

(a) Merchant intentionally interferes with PennyLime's collection of the Specified Percentage of Payment Transactions;
(b) Merchant breaches any covenant or representation in this Agreement;
(c) Any representation in this Agreement proves materially false when made;
(d) Merchant attempts to revoke or stop the ACH Authorization without PennyLime's prior consent;
(e) Merchant ceases operations, sells substantially all assets, or dissolves without PennyLime's prior written consent;
(f) Merchant changes banks, processors, or payout destinations without PennyLime's prior written consent;
(g) Merchant uses an additional or alternate bank account for Payment Transactions without PennyLime's prior written consent;
(h) Merchant files for bankruptcy or has an involuntary petition filed against it;
(i) Merchant has two (2) consecutive ACH debit failures attributable to non-sufficient funds or stop-payment orders;
(j) Any ACH debit returns with NACHA codes **R02, R05, R07, R08, R10, R29, or R51**;
(k) Merchant defaults under any other agreement with PennyLime.

**7.2 Remedies.** Upon an Event of Default, PennyLime may, at its sole option and in any combination:

(a) Increase the Specified Percentage to **100% of incoming Payment Transactions** until the full Purchased Receivables amount has been delivered;
(b) Declare the full unpaid Purchased Receivables amount, plus all fees and costs (including reasonable attorneys' fees and court costs), immediately due and payable;
(c) Initiate legal action to enforce its rights under this Agreement;
(d) Debit any of Merchant's bank accounts wherever situated by ACH or RCC for amounts due;
(e) Exercise any rights as a secured party under the Uniform Commercial Code, as described in Section 10;
(f) Notify any third-party processor or platform of the receivables sale and instruct payments to be redirected to a PennyLime-controlled account.

These remedies are cumulative and in addition to any others available at law or in equity.

**7.3 Notice obligations.** Merchant must notify PennyLime in writing within **24 hours** of any bankruptcy filing affecting Merchant, and at least **seven (7) days** in advance of any sale of substantially all of Merchant's assets.

### 12. GOVERNING LAW, VENUE, JURY WAIVER, AND ARBITRATION

**12.1 Governing law.** This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Florida, without regard to its conflict-of-laws principles.

**12.2 Venue.** Any court proceeding arising from or related to this Agreement (other than arbitration under Section 12.4) must be brought in the state or federal courts located in **Miami-Dade County, Florida** (the **"Acceptable Forum"**). Merchant submits to the jurisdiction of that forum and waives any objection to it. If proceedings are filed elsewhere, Merchant waives any right to oppose PennyLime's motion to transfer them to the Acceptable Forum. Service of process by certified mail to the addresses on this Agreement is sufficient.

**12.3 Jury trial waiver.** **THE PARTIES KNOWINGLY AND VOLUNTARILY WAIVE THE RIGHT TO TRIAL BY JURY IN ANY DISPUTE ARISING FROM OR RELATED TO THIS AGREEMENT.** Each Party acknowledges making this waiver after consultation with counsel of its choice (or having had the opportunity to do so).

**12.4 Arbitration.** At the election of either Party (the **"Requesting Party"**), any dispute arising from or related to this Agreement will be resolved by binding arbitration administered by the **American Arbitration Association ("AAA")** under its Consumer Arbitration Rules then in effect.

- The Requesting Party must first send the other Party written notice of intent to arbitrate by certified mail.
- If the dispute is not resolved within 30 days of that notice, either Party may initiate AAA proceedings.
- PennyLime will reimburse Merchant's reasonable filing fees and bear AAA administrative and arbitrator fees, except where the arbitrator finds Merchant's claim frivolous (Fed. R. Civ. P. 11(b) standard).
- If the arbitrator awards Merchant relief equal to or greater than what Merchant requested, PennyLime will reimburse Merchant's reasonable attorneys' fees.

**12.5 Class action waiver.** **THE PARTIES WAIVE ANY RIGHT TO BRING OR PARTICIPATE IN A CLASS, COLLECTIVE, OR REPRESENTATIVE PROCEEDING.** All claims must be brought in individual capacity only. The arbitrator may not consolidate claims of multiple persons. If this class waiver is held unenforceable for any claim, the arbitration clause as a whole is void as to that claim and the dispute must proceed in court.

**12.6 Opt-out from arbitration.** Merchant may opt out of the arbitration and class waiver provisions by sending written notice to PennyLime by certified mail within **14 days** after the Acceptance Date, to:

> Attn: Legal — Arbitration Opt-Out

Opting out does not affect any other provision of this Agreement.
