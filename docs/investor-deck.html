Risks & Mitigations

Key Risks —
Identified and Mitigated

Regulatory Risk
State-by-state lending regulations are complex and evolving. MCA disclosure laws expanding.
Mitigation: Compliance-first approach. Phase 1-2 require no lending license. Legal counsel from day one. Lead gen and marketplace model avoids most lending regulations.
Credit / Default Risk
Self-employed borrowers have higher default rates (8-18% for personal loans). Income volatility is the #1 predictor.
Mitigation: ML underwriting with multi-platform income data reduces defaults 20-30%. Phase 3 only — by then we have extensive borrower data. Conservative LTV/DTI limits.
Competition from Incumbents
Large lenders (CrossCountry, Angel Oak) could build direct-to-consumer brands. Gig platforms could build lending in-house.
Mitigation: Incumbents are wholesale-focused; consumer brand building is different DNA. Our SEO/content moat compounds. Platform partnerships make us a channel, not a competitor.
Economic Downturn
Recession could increase defaults and reduce loan demand. Gig economy could contract.
Mitigation: Self-employment actually grows in recessions (people get laid off and freelance). Non-QM grew through 2023-2024 rate shock. Lead gen model is recession-resilient.
Capital Requirements
Phase 3 lending requires significant capital for loan origination and balance sheet.
Mitigation: Phased approach delays capital needs. Phase 1-2 revenue funds growth. Warehouse lending facilities and securitization available for Phase 3 (proven by Sprout, Angel Oak).
SEO / Google Algorithm Risk
Google algorithm changes could affect organic traffic.
Mitigation: Diversified acquisition (social, partnerships, paid). E-E-A-T compliance. High-quality content with real lending expertise. Multiple traffic channels by Year 2.
